About Dr. Camille Pagniello

Dr. Camille Pagniello is a School of Ocean and Earth Science and Technology (SOEST) Early Career Fellow and Assistant Researcher at the Hawaiʻi Institute of Marine Biology, University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa. Her research interests lie at the intersection of engineering, data science, oceanography, and marine biology.

Dr. Camille has led several research and engineering projects, developing underwater acoustic and optical imaging systems as well as algorithms to study the sounds of commercially and recreationally important fish species within marine protected areas. She is currently leading a data science project to develop an oceanographic atlas to show the “state of the ocean” over the past 20 years using a large dataset collected by marine top predators equipped with ocean sensors.

In addition to her research, Dr. Camille has been an active member of the Marine Technology Society (MTS) and The Oceanography Society (TOS), serving in multiple leadership positions. She is AAUS Scientific Diver, and regularly dives for scientific research purposes.
